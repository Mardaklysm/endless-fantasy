# Gameplay Systems

## Overworld Movement

- The world is a 96x64 semantic archipelago grid generated by `src/world/semantic/` and adapted through `src/world/worldGenerator.ts`.
- New Game creates a fresh random world seed. Saves persist `worldSeed`, and Load Game regenerates the exact same world from that seed.
- The same seed reproduces the same islands, POIs, shallow-water markers, roads, and dungeon layouts; different seeds should visibly change island shapes and placement.
- Player position is `worldPos`.
- Movement is tile-center based with arrow keys/WASD: each accepted direction starts a one-tile step from the current tile center to the next tile center.
- Holding a direction continues smoothly tile-by-tile; releasing finishes the current step and stops with the leader's feet/shadow anchored on the destination tile center.
- Shift keeps faster exploration movement, but still advances one completed tile step at a time.
- Visual position interpolates only during the active step. Gameplay position, encounters, location entry, town exits, and dungeon triggers commit when the destination tile is reached.
- Exploration collision checks the destination tile center, not the full character sprite rectangle.
- Normal terrain visuals use a generated semantic mask/field background texture with deep ocean, shallow-water halos, beaches, grass/sand/ice fills, coastline foam, and softened biome edges. The 64 classified `atlas_v3` tile IDs in `src/data/worldTiles.ts` remain for semantic compatibility, debug raw-tile mode, fallback drawing, movement cost, and tags.
- World collision uses semantic walkability from `isWorldPositionWalkable(world, x, y)`: deep/shallow water, lakes, and mountain overlays block walking. Normal trees, normal forest clusters, and dense forest clusters are `softTerrain` overlays for now and remain walkable. Roads/rivers are visual-only overlays, and POI cells are tagged for interaction policy while still supporting the current step-on-location behavior.
- Deep water, lava, volcanoes, and blocked mountain tiles are not walkable. Boat travel does not make raw water walkable; harbors teleport between island harbor positions.
- Grass, desert/dryland, snow/ice ground, dark ground, gravel, and valid POI approach terrain are walkable.
- Generated overworld edges are deep water, and land must not touch the outer map border.
- The player sprite uses the same compact doubled map size on the world map, in towns, and in dungeons; this is visual only and does not change collision or movement.
- The `semantic_campaign_archipelago_world` generator creates four named major islands from the campaign profile: Greenhaven (starter grass/forest), Coralreach (sand/coastal trade), Frostmere (snow/ice shrine), and Highspire (mixed highland/mountain ruins), plus 3-6 seeded minor islands.
- The semantic generator computes land/water masks, island IDs, distance-driven shallow water and beach bands, grass/sand/ice biomes, elevation/ridges, river paths, road graph, theme-capped mountain ranges, passable forest clusters, overlay collision policies, POIs, harbors, and walkability before adapting visuals to the active `atlas_v3`, `pier_atlas`, and `world_objects` runtime assets.
- Each island has a town, harbor, at least one dungeon, roads between important points, and several landmarks such as shipwrecks, shrines, hidden chests, monster nests, ruins, caves, resource nodes, merchants, or ancient doors. Non-town generated POIs carry object overlay IDs so their visible marker matches their gameplay kind. One-shot landmarks go silent after being discovered so walking through their footprints does not repeat "already searched" messages; secret merchants can still reopen their shop.
- Overworld locations use larger 3x3-ish footprints for entry and forgiving interaction. Normal POIs are validated against existing clear land and no longer paint rectangular terrain patches under themselves; harbors and shipwrecks are the coastal exceptions. Visual overlays render smaller within their footprint so object art does not dominate the tile scale.
- Towns/dungeons/gates/final locations enter automatically when crossed. Harbor and landmark footprints are overworld interactions that open travel, rewards, shops, healing, lore, or optional fights.
- Town south gates can exit when stepping into the exit tile or pressing south while already standing on the gate tile.
- Town interactions check the facing tile first, then the nearby forgiving service/NPC fallback.
- Menus, dialogues, and battles block exploration movement.

## Harbor Travel

- Harbors are generated on or near coasts and exposed as `harbor` POIs.
- Harbor travel uses `TravelDestination` data with `destinationIslandId`, display name, cost, and optional unlock flag.
- Greenhaven to Coralreach is available immediately for 10 gold. The Coralreach dungeon boss drops the Chartered Compass and unlocks Frostmere and Highspire chart routes.
- Travel deducts gold, sets `currentIslandId`, moves `worldPos` to the destination harbor arrival tile, marks visit/unlock flags, saves, and shows a sailing message.
- If the party lacks gold, travel is denied with a passage-cost message.

## Random Encounters

- Random encounters occur on dangerous world terrain and inside dungeons when settings allow.
- Encounter setting can be toggled in the menu.
- Encounter counter decrements only when the derived logical tile changes; forests/hills are more dangerous.
- World encounter tables are selected from generated tile encounter family plus island/biome hooks. Generated roads suppress encounters; forest hooks use forest enemies, Coralreach sand uses sand/water enemies, Frostmere snow/mountains use hill-style danger, and Highspire mountains use final/dangerous enemies.
- Dungeon encounter tables are defined per dungeon.
- F9 debug menu can start an encounter for testing.

## Battle System

- Battles use individual initiative turns.
- Commands: Attack, Skill, Magic, Item, Defend, Run.
- At battle start and at the start of each initiative cycle, every living party member and enemy rolls initiative.
- Current formula: party initiative is `speed * 10 + random 0-12 + floor(luck / 2)`; enemy initiative is `speed * 10 + random 0-12`.
- Highest initiative acts first.
- Only the current party member gets a command menu.
- Player attacks, skills, spells, items, defend, and run attempts pass through a short resolving animation on that actor's turn, then hand off immediately to the next actor without an extra post-action wait.
- Enemies plan a visible intent before acting. The intent label appears above enemies and in the target panel. On the enemy turn, the planned move resolves, then the next intent is planned.
- After every living combatant in the current order has had a turn or been skipped, a new initiative cycle is rolled.
- The game does not use an all-party action queue round system.
- Battle presentation now uses v2 scene backdrops, enemies on the left, normalized fighter/priest/wizard class-sheet party battlers on the right, and lower panels for target/log, commands, and party status. The small redundant party head portraits are not drawn on the battlefield.
- Party attack animations use the class-sheet `attack_windup_left` then `attack_release_left` frames during the existing lunge timing. Battle idle uses the left-facing walk frame, while enemy PNG battlers are horizontally flipped so enemies on the left face right toward the party.
- Battle backgrounds are selected by dungeon first, then overworld terrain for random encounters.
- Enemy moves support attack, damage, status, defend, heal, charge, and steal-gold behaviors.
- Dead attack targets retarget to a living enemy.
- Boss battles cannot be fled.
- Defend is applied immediately and lasts until that character's next turn starts.
- Poison, burn, and bleed tick at the start of the affected actor's turn.
- Sleep/stun are checked at the start of the affected actor's turn and skip that turn when active.
- Ward/starveil/silence/weakness/guarded/charged/burn/bleed durations tick at the end of the affected actor's own turn.
- Victory awards XP and gold; all party members receive XP by default, including fallen members. Random fights can also drop small item rewards.
- Game over occurs when all party members fall.

## Shops, Inns, And Clinics

Each town has item stock, weapon stock, armor stock, spell stock, inn price, clinic price, NPCs, and optional arrival behavior.

- Inns restore HP/spell charges and save.
- Clinics revive fallen characters for gold.
- Shops buy items, gear, and spells.
- Town/city interiors render floor and wall tiles from the opaque `dungeon_atlas`, with atlas-backed shop pads under the existing service icon sprites. Interior atlas picks are weighted toward one base tile with sparse crack/moss/debris accents so floors do not become checkerboards.

Shop UI is menu-based text only.

## Dungeons, Bosses, And Progression

Dungeons are data-driven in `dungeons()` but their floors come from `src/world/dungeonGenerator.ts`:

- Mossy Cave -> Root Relic.
- Coralreach Ruins -> Tide Relic and Chartered Compass route unlocks.
- Stonefall Keep/`ashenKeep` -> Flame Relic.
- Skyglass Tower -> Gale Relic.
- Eclipse Spire -> final boss.

Each dungeon has two deterministic procedural 22x14 floors generated from `worldSeed + dungeonId + tier`, with rooms, corridors, chests, stairs, a switch/gate puzzle, a boss tile, boss intro text, and reward text. Runtime dungeon entry and stair travel search the generated floor for `E` and `S` markers instead of using fixed coordinates, and save loading snaps invalid dungeon positions back to a valid marker-adjacent walkable tile.

Dungeon floors, walls, gates, stairs, exits, chests, switches, and boss relic seals render from the active opaque `dungeon_atlas` sheet with theme-specific cells for Mossy Cave, Coralreach Ruins/Tide Shrine, Stonefall Keep/`ashenKeep`, Skyglass Tower, and Eclipse Spire. Dungeon atlas picks are weighted toward a theme base tile with sparse accents, unused solid-fill `#` cells render as void unless they border carved floor, and small 22x14 dungeon maps are centered in the viewport. Older individual dungeon PNGs and generated drawing remain fallback paths. Opened chest art is wired through `openedChests`.

Progression flags:

- Greenhaven harbor opens Coralreach for 10 gold immediately.
- Coralreach Ruins boss drops Chartered Compass and unlocks Frostmere/Highspire travel.
- Flame and Tide help unlock Skyglass Tower.
- Four relics open Starfall Gate.
- Gate/skyship allow Eclipse Spire.

## Save/Load

Saves use browser `localStorage`.

Saved data includes:

- Party state
- Inventory and gear bag
- Gold
- Current island id
- Positions
- Current town/dungeon/floor
- Story/travel flags
- Discovered points of interest
- Opened chests
- Puzzle flags
- Defeated bosses
- Cleared dungeons
- Settings
- Encounter counter

Older saves are normalized for missing world seed, travel flags, discovered POIs, cleared dungeons, inventory defaults, and skill cooldowns. Known gap: save data has no explicit version field.

## Known Gaps

- No automated browser E2E or balance simulation tests.
- No visual spell/attack animation timing system yet.
- Mouse menu support is minimal.
- Item/equipment/relic icons are loaded but not yet displayed in menus/HUD.
- UI panel skin now uses the v2 compact window frame with live Phaser text; reference UI windows with sample text remain source-only.
- Save schema migration/versioning is not implemented.
- Balance has only manual smoke-test coverage so far.
